Listening to Karky gobinath youtube video - [link](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QRr3yDuMoKg) #podcast

Karky's perspective is impressive. It sparks interest in my native language, Tamil. I was amazed by his logical approach to lyrics writing. Everyone used to acknowledge lyrics as artistic expression, and AI can't replace it. However, he doesn't entirely agree with it. Naturally, humans are superior to machines because humans seek more experience. But an artist can become a great artist using AI according to Karky.

I will detest Tamil until the end of my education. I struggled with both English and Tamil. However, learning Tamil seems more pointless. Perhaps the staff and school system's emphasis on memorization of poems is to blame. We used to memorize poems in school that were punctuated exactly like textbooks. As I mature, my perspective on poetry completely shifts. There is a lot of interest in my language after listening to a lot of Tamil music. Language should be admired rather than graded.

I believe that Tamil poetry is under danger these days.
I feel so bad about current Tamil movie song lyrics. Hiphop Aadhi and Sai Abhiyankar have recently released songs. I sense a threat for poetry in songs. I'm glad Na. Muthukumar passed without hearing these. The music video for Kabir Vasuki's '*Firangi Pani*' has been released. I'm irritated after listening to Kabir's interview lately. He has been hailed and his lyrics have been glorified. He claims to be an artist and uses his own gaslighting poetry to support this claim. In my opinion, artists are similar to Karky, who works with simple logic and yet producing great art. Actually they don't portray
themself as an artist.

I'm not being political. One of the things about Tamil Nadu politics that I grateful for is the significance given to Tamil. I'm happy that the three-language policy is no longer in effect.

I'm going to explore with his Olingoa (Transliterator), I will try to develop a tool. 👍👍
